Starting a tech company today is often too expensive and heavy.

A good idea shouldn't need a huge budget to reach users. But founders often get stuck. They build systems that are too complex, get distracted by new tech trends, and pay for servers before they even have their first user.

They spend too much time building and not enough time validating.

We started Cyberk to fix this.
We want to make tech startups simple and effective.

To go far, we focus deeply on the basics.
We are here to help you master new technologies. Instead of letting you guess and test many different tools, we do the hard work first. We learn them inside out and apply them to real projects. We practice until it becomes second nature. This means you won’t have to pay for beginner mistakes.

Every idea is unique, but the systems behind them are often the same. Over the years, we have gathered these common parts to build a solid foundation.

When you bring us a new idea, we never start from zero. We have already solved common problems like databases and scaling. All of our time goes into building what makes your product different.
An MVP that usually takes three months, we build in thirty days.

We don't write long status reports on weekends, either.
We give you the product to use every morning.
Any misunderstandings are fixed within 24 hours. Good code doesn't come from a QA team at the very end. It comes from the developer testing the feature dozens of times before you even see it.

The system must always be ready. It should be small and almost free when you have no users, but able to grow naturally when many people visit at once. That is a good architecture. Simple and natural.

Starting a business is risky enough. Technology should not be another risk.

We take care of the tech.
So you can focus on what really matters: finding your market and building a good business.

That is why Cyberk exists.
